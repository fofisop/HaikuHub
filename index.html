What is a Haiku?

A haiku is a style of poetry originating in Japan in which there are really only two rules:

1.There are only three lines

2.The structure of a haiku is 5-7-5, also known as 5 syllabules for the first and last lines, and seven syllabulles for the second line.

Tips & Tools

Read the page to find out some cool tricks on how to become a haiku champion! Check out our Haiku verification tool as well!

  • Don't let the rules rule you. If you are making a haiku and the syllable count is off, it could still count as a version of haiku!
  • Describe things in as much detail as you can. Even through haikus are short, it doesn't mean that they should be bland! So make sure to keep all of your senses on point when writing a haiku.
  • Before starting to write your haiku, consider your theme, and jot down about twenty words that relate to your theme. Then go over them again, and string the words together to make a haiku!
  • Haikus don't nessacarily have to make sense. As long as there is emotion, rythym, and your artistic talent, it will work!
  • Read your haiku aloud to check its rythm. Awkward, but it helps!
